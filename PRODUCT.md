# Product

## Register

brand

PRODUCT.md governs the site-wide identity; tool-page work should read both this file and the product register reference.

## Users

Two audiences, one brand:

- **Professionals** (HR managers, project managers, freelancers, finance teams) who arrive via search for a specific utility — payroll period calculator, business days counter, sprint date planner — and need fast, accurate answers with zero friction.
- **Curious explorers** (general public, science and space enthusiasts, the chronologically obsessed) who come for the Realms — relativistic travel simulators, black hole gravity playgrounds, life mosaic generators, cosmic horror clocks — and stay because nothing else feels like this.

Both groups share one thing: they came because time means something to them right now. The professional needs precision. The explorer wants to feel the weight of time. The brand serves both without condescending to either.

## Product Purpose

The God of Time is a dual-purpose time platform. It offers 100+ precision utility calculators (date math, HR & payroll, project management, global time zones, health & lifecycle) alongside immersive "Fun Realms" — interactive experiences that let users explore time through cosmological, biological, paradoxical, and whimsical lenses.

Success looks like: a payroll manager bookmarks the timecard tool and later finds themselves computing how many heartbeats they've lived. A student discovers the relativistic travel simulator and shares it; their friend uses the sleep calculator the next morning.

## Brand Personality

**Commanding, curious, strange.**

- **Commanding**: The name "God of Time" is not modest. The site holds this position with confidence — it doesn't hedge, it doesn't apologize for its scale or ambition.
- **Curious**: Genuinely fascinated by time across every dimension — cosmic, biological, professional, absurd. The curiosity is contagious; it draws both audiences in.
- **Strange**: The Realms are genuinely weird in the best sense. The brand doesn't flatten the strangeness to seem more approachable. It leans in.

Voice: dry, confident, occasionally cosmic in scope. Not snarky, not warm-and-fuzzy, not clinical.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS / Notion-clone**: Cream or near-white background, Inter or DM Sans everywhere, icons-in-rounded-cards grid, "Calculate" in a blue pill button. This is the single biggest trap — a time tool site that looks like every other tool site.
- **NASA / government utility**: Cold, clinical, functional-only. Correct information in a grey table is not the same as an experience. The tools can be precise without being sterile.
- **Hypnotic / overwhelming dark**: Glow effects, blur saturation, animated particles on everything, colors that vibrate against each other. Cosmic scale doesn't require visual noise. The dark aesthetic should feel like the void, not like a rave flyer.
- **Childish / gamified**: Cartoon planet emojis as primary UI, rainbow gradients, confetti animations. The Realms are playful but they're not juvenile. The platform takes time seriously even when it's being strange.

## Design Principles

1. **Dual register, one voice.** The same commanding, curious, strange identity must feel at home on both a relativistic travel simulator and a payroll period planner. Tool pages go clean and precise, not clinical and anonymous.

2. **Strange by choice, not by accident.** "Strange" in the personality means intentional. Every unusual decision — a retro power switch for the theme toggle, an emoji avatar cluster in the nav, a 4px hard shadow — should have a reason. Strangeness as decoration is noise; strangeness as voice is identity.

3. **Scale without spectacle.** The name invokes cosmic scale. The anti-references rule out visual noise. Carry weight through restraint and precision — dark fields of space, exact typography, deliberate accent color. Not through blur, glow, or motion overload.

4. **Both audiences in five seconds.** A professional who lands on the payroll calculator and a space nerd who lands on the Realms index must both feel "this was made for me" immediately. Neither should feel like a guest in the other's space.

5. **Dark as identity, not as default.** The dark-first theme is a deliberate stance — time, the cosmos, the void. It's not dark because "tools look cool dark." Own the darkness; make it carry meaning.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal compliance target. Ship with care: sufficient contrast on all text, full respect for `prefers-reduced-motion`, keyboard navigation on interactive Realms and tool inputs.
